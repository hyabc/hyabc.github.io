Why China's Writing Education is a Disaster

China’s high school writing education is a completely disaster for students. Consider these writing samples which are typical essays that teachers encourage students to write. These essays have the following issues that exist in most essays conforming to Chinese high school writing requirements.

Nondeterministic argument

Nondeterministic argument is definitely a nightmare for readers. In the introduction paragraph of sample 1, the student writes life should be “ordinary” and then refutes this argument by signaling “不过”. Now you will naturally expect that the author believes the opposite side. Surprisingly, after paragraphs full of swinging between two opposite arguments and off-topic discussion, the student finally concludes that life should be both “ordinary” and “not ordinary”. Now I feel I am fooled by an essay entirely discussing $T \vee \neg T$.

Obsolete language

Chinese language contains many outdated words that few people use or even know their meanings. These words can be replaced by simple and modern words that most people understand, so they should be deprecated. However, the usage of obsolete language is encouraged in high school writing education. Any part of the sample document is full of obsolete words, such as “究其根本”, “正因如此”, “事有两面，难得定论” (another example of nondeterministic argument), “撷取”, and many many others. Teachers should not encourage students to write articles full of outdated and fancy words that convey no meaningful ideas and scare off readers.

Opinion without evidence

This issue is also widespread in compliant Chinese high school essays. These essays keep giving personal opinion without evidence or explanation. (Update: the example is removed because it is not characteristic enough.)

Writing should be an enjoyable activity. However, many high schools try hard to educate students into writing in this horrible way, which is worse than no education at all. Unfortunately, many students learned to write unreadable article with nondeterministic arguments.
